Welcome to our experiment.

In this experiment you will perform four tasks. They involve making decisions about probability, a survey about your experiences, and some math problems. More detailed instructions will be provided if you decide to continue.

We will also be checking how well you understand the instructions and the experiment.

The results of this research will only be used for scientific purposes. The results can be presented at conferences and can be published in scientific journals. The results may also be published in newspapers, promotional material used by the university, or on the website of the university department. Finally, the data may be made available publically via the Open Science Framework.

The data will never be shown in such a way that you could be personally identified, so it will include a randomly generated code instead of your Turk ID. No personal information will ever be shared or linked to the data that you provide. You may withdraw your data at any time prior to publication. If you wish to withdraw your data, please email us with either your Turk ID or the completion code you'll get at the end of the experiment. If you take part in more than one of our surveys, we may combine data across surveys, though we will do so using an anonymous key so the data will not be linked to your Turk ID. If you are dissatisfied with any of these terms, please contact us via email (justin.sulik@rhul.ac.uk).

Participation in this experiment is voluntary. To collect your full payment, you will need to enter a completion code, which you will receive after the completion of the experiment. Although you are free to withdraw your participation at any time, if you do not complete the experiment after consenting, you will not receive a completion code and will be unable to claim your payment.
